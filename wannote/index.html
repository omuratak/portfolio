Dog Health App
Design

UI/UX Design

Project Summary

Background

I adopted a dog in 2020, and have wanted to better manage my dog's health. In particular, all of the communication around my dog's health (e.g. vaccinations, vet visits) was paper-based, which felt inconvenient. Hence, I wondered if I could make my dog's health management more "digitalized".

Target

Users who have pet dogs (and vets). This time I focused on pet owners instead of vets.

Issues surrounding pet owners

Everything about my dog's health is paper-based (e.g. vaccination records, prescriptions for hospital visits, etc.), and hence keeping everything under control can be a bit tricky. For example, when users take their dogs to different vets, the medical records are not shared among vets, so it is inconvenient for the the user to have to explain the situation time and again. It is also difficult to simultaneously take notes while listening to the vet's advice.

Issues surrounding vets (hypothetical)

(*hypothesized since I have not been able to interview vets) When a patient sees multiple vets, there is no coordination / information sharing among the vets.

Solution

I designed a mobile application that digitizes dog health management (e.g. vaccination records, vet visit history, medical records, and daily health records) to facilitate collaboration between owners and vets.

Context

I worked on this project as a side-project, and thus I worked on all of the research & design.

Process

I followed the human centered design approach. I first conducted user interviews to understand behaviors before/during/after a vet visit, defined the design problem, and then designed and tested the solution.

1. Understand and Define

First, I interviewed 2 friends who owned dogs, and learned about their recent experiences taking their dogs to the vet. Then, based on that, I created a persona and user journey (AS-IS).

In addition, I created an ideal user journey (TO-BE), in order to highlight what the experience should be.

Based on the insights, I decided on the below features:

  1. A memo feature to write down their dogs' health condition
  2. A reservation feature to search and reserve a vet visit
  3. A feature to share medical questionnaires with the vet beforehand
  4. A feature to review details around treatments and prescriptions

2. Design and Evaluate

Based on the features, I then created the information architecture in an object-oriented manner. I first created objects,

Given the features I decided on, I defined the information architecture in an object-oriented manner. We first created objects, their properties, and actions. After that, we organized the relationships between the objects and their views (collection vs. single).

Based on the information architecture, I sketched a rough concept/prototype. Using this sketch, I conducted a quick & easy concept test to confirm whether the user flow had any issues. After that, I created the visual system, and combined that with the paper sketch to create a hi-fi prototype. For this project, I focused on one user flow: Account registration → Memo → Reservation → Change reservation → Check treatment details → Check my page.

I used the prototype and conducted a usability test. I was able to confirm that there were no big issues with the overall user flow, aside from a few minor usability issues. The user evaluated the design as "Easy and intuitive to use."

See the final prototype

Reflection

For this project, I tried out an object-oriented UI design approach to minimize modality. As a result, I was able to minimize the number of screens that were required for the design, as well as make the UI user-friendly and intuitive.

If we were to actually launch this product, it would not be easy, as I anticipate that many problems would arise in digitizing the conventional workflow on the vet's side. Albeit, it was rewarding to be able to create something that I find to be extremely helpful for pet owners.
